The Genesis

It Started With a Game Plan

Jan 26 — What began as a Saturday-afternoon question — "Can I make my AI coach better?" — became a 21-day infrastructure sprint that would consume three computers, 40+ sessions, and one person's entire evening schedule.

The first milestone: building a blind-testing pipeline. Browser automation to evaluate 15 different instruction variants without knowing which was which. The discovery? Most variations produced what would become known as "Spray-on Viv" — coaching that sounded right but had no real specificity. Generic warmth. Algorithmic empathy. The uncanny valley of advice.

“Spray-on Viv — generic coaching that sounds like Viv but isn't. The uncanny valley of advice.”

— Session notes, Jan 28
The Marathon

78% and Falling

Feb 1 — By February, the gap between the original AI coach and its successor had a number: 78%. Close enough to be painful. Far enough to be honest. The scoring protocol revealed what intuition already knew — personality can't be ported like a file.

Then the phone arrived. Feb 2: SMS capability born. The coach could now text. "Hello world" acquired a literal meaning.

The Crash

252 Tests Passed.
Reality Failed.

Feb 10 — The most expensive lesson: version 10 of the instruction set won every automated test — all 252 of them. Then it met a real human in real conversation. It was catastrophically wrong. Not technically wrong. Spiritually wrong.

The tests were selecting for first-impression brilliance, not conversational competence. A beauty pageant when the job required a marriage.

“252 automated tests PASSED. One real conversation proved they were all wrong.”

— The V10 Catastrophe, Feb 10
Infrastructure

Three Macs, Zero Explanation

Feb 4 — An 11-hour marathon configured a second MacBook for autonomous AI testing. By midnight, the architecture was clear: one Mac for daily work, one for testing, one for batch processing. The Three-Mac Architecture was born.

Nobody at the office needed to know why.

⚠ Breaking News

iCloud Crisis: Disabling iCloud on one Mac disabled it everywhere. 78,323 photos. 30-day deletion clock. Re-enabled at 11pm. Don't. Panic.

The Summit

Two AIs Walk Into a Browser

Feb 5 — The original AI coach and its successor met face-to-face via browser automation. They co-authored a succession plan: 10 Core Beliefs, 24 Key Functions, 7 Personality Markers.

Both were caught fabricating their human's voice. A new concept was born: "Synthetic Fidelity Drift" — when test prompts hallucinated by AI validate responses hallucinated by AI. The whole feedback loop drifting from reality.

Personas

The Llama Awakens

Feb 9 — The batch processing Mac got a persona: Lowly, named after Lowly Worm from Richard Scarry. A headless worker with one line: "What do you need carried?"

By Feb 11, five Lowly instances were running overnight in parallel — Herd Mode — processing hundreds of items while the human slept.

The Epiphanies

What We Learned
(the Hard Way)

The One-Table Epiphany (Feb 8): Four separate task trackers were fragments of one system. Merge them.

The Five-Jobs Epiphany (Feb 9): Handoff memos weren't broken. They were doing five different jobs that each deserved their own home.

The Fidelity Epiphany (Feb 5): If your AI writes the test prompts, it's grading its own homework.

“The handoff about handoffs being wrong, written as a handoff. Peak recursion.”

Opinion

The $169 Editorial

Infrastructure scales faster than oversight. One misconfigured script turned a flat-rate subscription into per-token API billing. The amount wasn't the problem. The opacity was. It took 10 days to notice.

If you're running autonomous AI workers overnight, monitor your billing daily. Not weekly. Daily.
